Using Briefly

Briefly is an AI side panel for your browser. You can speak or type requests, inspect the captured page context, and turn that into structured output you can copy, refine, export, save, or route into your tools.

Basic flow

1. Open the side panel – click the Briefly icon or use the keyboard shortcut defined in Chrome.

2. Capture or type – click the mic button or hold Space while the panel is focused, or type directly into the command composer.

3. Pick a recipe – choose a workflow such as bug report, PR review, test plan, spec, release notes, or customer reply.

4. Transcribe – if you used voice, audio is sent directly from the extension to your selected Speech-to-Text provider (OpenAI, Google, or ElevenLabs).

5. Generate – the request plus selected page context is sent directly from the extension to an LLM (OpenAI), which produces the result.

6. Use the output – copy it, refine it, export it, save it to the library, or use Review & Send to route it to Notion, GitHub, Jira, Linear, Slack, Confluence, or a webhook.

Turning ideas into prompts and specs

You can open Briefly on any page (even an empty one), click the mic, and talk through what you want to build. Briefly will:

  • Transcribe your speech into text.
  • Classify your intent (for example prompt generation, task extraction, user story, or documentation).
  • Generate a structured output you can use as a prompt, spec, or backlog.

For long ideas, it works best to record in shorter segments (30–90 seconds) and use the Refine controls to iterate.

History and prompt library

  • Every run is saved to History in the main view.
  • You can restore a past item to bring back its transcript, intent, and output.
  • Use the ⭐ button to add important outputs to the Prompt Library for quick reuse.

Integrations

In Settings → Integrations you can provide the tokens and target IDs needed for Notion, GitHub, Jira, Linear, Slack, Confluence, or a custom webhook. Once configured, the Review & Send flow lets you preview the payload and route the latest output into those tools.

Microphone and permissions

  • Chrome will ask for microphone permission the first time you record. Choose Allow.
  • If you blocked it earlier, go to chrome://settings/content/microphone and allow the Briefly extension.
  • You can clear all local data (history, settings, keys) from Settings → Data & Privacy → Clear All Local Data.

Troubleshooting

  • “Microphone Access Denied” – check Chrome’s mic settings and macOS Privacy & Security → Microphone.
  • “Processing Failed” – verify your OpenAI key in Settings → API Keys and that you have access to the configured models.
  • No output – ensure your STT provider key is set (Google / ElevenLabs) or transcription models are allowed for your OpenAI project.
